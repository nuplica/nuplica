Computational Design Lab, Columbia University

Formed by a multidisciplinary team at a world-renowned research institution, the lab needed an identity capable of communicating about its and research efforts across multiple media in a global context.

It was decided early on in the development process that the identity should convey the relationship between the algorithmic and design-related aspects of the research.

Context

The lab engages with the scientific community through peer reviewed publications, talks, conferences, courses, and development projects executed by its members.

Production at the lab ranges from code libraries and prototypes, to multimedia projects like motion graphics, visual blends, synthetically generated imagery, and design tooling.

It also leads outreach efforts by organizing conferences related to the intersection of design and computation
