Voxon

Inspired by the research of Prof. Hod Lipson at the Creative Machines Lab at Columbia University, Voxon provides an experimental interface for sketching soft robots that have life-like locomotion and can displace themselves through 3D space.

The environment was created using WebGL to build a very simple physics engine that integrates the forces acting over the collection of voxels that form the body of the robot.

Click on the image above to try the Voxon demo

Contex

Three-dimensional user interfaces provide a medium for experimental creativity and allow us to simulate life by defining virtual materials and environments.

With the advent of WebGL, browsers gained the capability to run such complex 3D environments and behaviors.

Voxel as a modular form factor

The demo uses voxels as a means to simplify the construction of the robots while maintaining enough flexibility so that the user is free to create complex three-dimensional structures.

The user can navigate by shifting the camera and pointing the mouse to an available slot for a voxel, and insert it with a simple click

Experimental takeaways

As a first foray into computer graphics using WebGL,Voxel clearly overpowers the framework’s capabilities. Yet, it lays the foundation for future experiments using more performant technologies.

Simple engines like these can speed up the production process for characters in video games and other digital media, and can open up three-dimensional design for a wider audience.
